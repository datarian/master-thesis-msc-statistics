4.2 Imputation

(ref:jupyter-4-imputation)

Several techniques were explored to impute missing values. In the end, a simple approach was chosen: Categorical features had a missing level added during encoding. All other features were imputed by their median value to account for the skewed distributions. See the corresponding Jupyter notebook13 for details.

Missing data before categorical encoding and median-imputation of numeric features is shown in Figure 4.1. The matrix displays the complete data set, with missing values indicated by white cells.

Figure 4.1: Data before imputation of numeric features. The big complete block at the center is the US census data.

The features with most and fewest missing values are shown in (Figure 4.2). It is not surprising to find the MONTHS_TO_DONATION features among those with most missing because few examples respond to the promotions and hence, don’t donate.

Figure 4.2: Features with most (left) and fewest (right) missing features.
